The biggest galaxies

And interesting facts about them

IC 1101

IC 1101 is the largest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical to lenticular and appears golden yellow in color. The brightest galaxy is located more than 1.04 billion light years from Earth.

Phoenix Cluster

Phoenix Cluster is one of the most solid galaxy groups in the universe. It is located around 5.7 billion light years from Earth. The elliptical galaxy is composed of medium and dark intra-group matter.

NGC 262

NGC 262 is located 202 million light years from Earth, which is one of the largest known spiral galaxies. The diameter of the galaxy is approximately 1.3 million light years.

NGC 4889

NGC 4889 is also famous as “Coma B”, a super giant elliptical galaxy located approximately 308 million light years from Earth. It was also discovered by British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.

NGC 4874

NGC 4874 also known as “Coma A” was discovered in 1785 by British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I. And it is 350 million light years away from Earth. It is a smooth, ball-shaped galaxy surrounded by a star circle.

A2261-BCG

A2261-BCG was located approximately 3 billion light years from Earth. It is elliptical in shape and was found by the Hubble Space Telescope. A2261-BCG is one million light years across.
